## Services & the WVIP protocol *(expands the intake's brief services list)*
The core offering is the **Wellspring Vision Improvement Protocol (WVIP)** — a comprehensive, patient-centered TCM program (created 1999). Modalities:
- **Acupuncture** — specialized points around the eyes and body to improve blood circulation and stimulate retinal cell regeneration.
- **Herbal Medicine** — custom Chinese herbal prescriptions to nourish the liver and kidneys (which govern eye health in TCM theory).
- **Eye Exercises** — guided eye-movement exercises and self-care to maintain/enhance results at home.
- **Supplementary Procedures** — additional in-clinic procedures selected per individual presentation.
- **Antioxidant Supplements** — research-supported supplementation to protect retinal cells from oxidative stress.
- **Diet** — TCM-aligned guidance emphasizing foods that nourish liver/kidney meridian energy.
- **Exercise** — moderate, consistent activity tailored to the patient's vision/mobility.
- **Eye Usage / Hygiene** — review of screen time, lighting, and protective eyewear habits.

**Program options:** Standard Program (10–20-day intensive) · Trial Program (10-day initial treatment for late-stage cases) · Customized Plans.
*Note: the intake listed services as "Acupuncture, Herbal Medicine, SightSage Supplement." The website presents the fuller RP-focused protocol above; SightSage supplements complement the herbal/supplement component.*

## Conditions treated
- **Retinitis Pigmentosa (RP)** — defined on-site as a group of rare genetic disorders involving breakdown/loss of retinal cells. Symptoms cited: night blindness, tunnel vision (progressive peripheral loss), color-vision loss. Motivating stat used: *"The majority of RP patients become legally blind before the age of 60."*
- **Choroideremia** is also referenced (patient Keith) as stabilized with acupuncture + herbal medicine.

## New-patient journey & intake process *(fills the blank "new-patient questions" field)*
A structured multi-phase journey:
1. **Online Consultation** — meet a Wellspring team member online; guidance on which medical reports to bring for Dr. Yu's review, plus help with visa applications, referrals, and travel logistics. Most patients arrive already diagnosed by an ophthalmologist.
2. **Evaluation / Intake (in Vancouver)** — Dr. Yu evaluates symptoms, health, and history; builds a personalized plan. Comprehensive baseline eye testing, including a simple marked-wall test measuring the farthest clear point in four directions.
3. **Initial / Intensive Program** — stay in Vancouver ~6–10 days (typical plan ~10 days / 30 treatments); 18–30 daily acupuncture treatments + custom herbal formulas + selected supplements.
4. **Follow-up / Maintenance** — go home with a 3-month supply of bespoke herbal tea + supplements; after 3 months get an exam + visual-field test from a local specialist; Dr. Yu reviews and refines, then the cycle repeats.

**Eligibility / who's a fit:** patients who can still read books have the strongest improvement potential; partial-vision non-readers may treat with limited results; light-perception-only patients have a very low chance. Earlier intervention = better odds. **No guarantee of improvement;** many see change within 3–6 months.
