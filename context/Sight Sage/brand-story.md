## Brand Story, Authority & Quality

*Source: SightSage (sightsage.com/en-ca) — brand-level pages. This captures how SightSage presents its own story and authority on its e-commerce site, which is distinct from the Wellspring Clinic.*

### Who They Are (Brand Identity & Mission)

SightSage positions itself as **"an innovative, people-focused brand of functional foods and nutritional supplements designed to enhance eyesight and overall wellness."** It is framed as a **Holistic Natural Health & Wellness** company centered on eye health in the digital age.

The mission is built around a **"prevention over treatment"** philosophy. The brand addresses vision problems caused by prolonged screen exposure — dry eyes, eye fatigue, blurry vision, and myopia — with the stated goal of reducing the risk of more serious conditions such as glaucoma and macular degeneration. A core part of the mission is making eye care **accessible and enjoyable as part of daily self-care routines**.

The brand describes itself as **"a purpose-driven, people-focused company"** that aims to make a positive difference **"in communities near and far,"** and notes it has built a customer base of **"thousands of people."**

**Core Values:** *"Integrity, Accountability, Simplicity, Compassion."*

**Product Philosophy:** SightSage combines **"Contemporary Nutrition Science with Traditional Chinese Medicine Wisdom"** to create evidence-based products, using **"100% natural whole superfood ingredients with nutrients in their best forms and no unnecessary additives."**

### Founder & Team Authority (as presented on SightSage)

**Dr. Weidong Yu — Founder and CEO**
- Described as **"one of Canada's leading Doctors of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), world-renowned for his successful treatment of retinal eye disorders."**
- Co-founder of the **Wellspring Clinic**; has successfully treated **over 1,000 patients with Retinitis Pigmentosa** — a rare eye disease "previously considered untreatable."
- Patient care also spans macular degeneration, myopia, presbyopia, dry eye disease, glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy, astigmatism, amblyopia, and viral optic neuritis.
- Brings **"20+ years of research and clinical practice"** in vision health and healing factors.
- Has practiced in **Belgium, Canada, the US, and China**, and holds a **California state license as an acupuncturist**.
- Has authored **over 30 publications** in medical literature.
- Stated personal mission: **"Sight for Humanity."**

**Kathy Yu / Kathy Zhang (KathyHealthTips) — Co-Founder**
- A **Registered Dietitian** and **certified acupuncturist**, "famous for sharing free health tips based on the ancient wisdom of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)."
- Brings **"more than 30 years of experience in TCM."**
- Co-manages the **Wellspring Clinic** with Dr. Yu in Vancouver, BC, specializing in Retinitis Pigmentosa and other vision conditions, as well as non-vision concerns (hearing loss, infertility, insomnia, weight management, skin health, joint pain, cancer support).

The brand frames its product development as **"backed by 40+ years of clinical research."**

> Note: The brand uses both "Kathy Yu" (homepage/About framing) and "Kathy Zhang" (Founder page) to refer to the co-founder. Authority claims (clinic, patient counts, publications) are presented on the SightSage brand site but are rooted in the founders' Wellspring Clinic practice.

### Clinical Validation & Credibility

- SightSage states it is **"currently undergoing a clinical trial with the University of Waterloo,"** presented as evidence of scientific validation and a commitment to evidence-based products.
- The brand references a registered study on **ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT05027087)**, indicating its research is logged on the official U.S. clinical-trials database.
- A dedicated **"Clinical Studies"** page is featured in the brand's "Our Mission" navigation.

### Manufacturing, Quality & Sourcing Claims

- **Natural ingredients:** Products are made with **"100% natural whole superfood ingredients"** with **"no unnecessary additives"** and a **"full spectrum of nutrition."** SightC specifically is described as **"made with seven 100% natural whole superfood ingredients."**
- **Sourcing:** **"Each ingredient is meticulously sourced for its renowned health-enhancing properties."** The brand strives to source ingredients **from where the ingredients naturally grow**, using natural herbs and **food-grade components**.
- **Manufacturing standard (GMP / NSF):** The manufacturing facility is certified to **Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP)** standards by **NSF**. Notably, the brand's FAQ addresses **"Why is NSF not displayed on your label?"** — meaning the GMP/NSF certification applies to the facility, but the NSF mark is not printed on product labels.
- **Safety positioning:** SightC is described as **"made with all natural ingredients and you shouldn't experience any side-effects."**
- The FAQ also covers ingredient sourcing, animal testing, and bottle recyclability as standing topics, signaling the brand's intent to address quality, ethics, and sustainability questions.

### Guarantees & Policies

- **30-Day Money-Back Guarantee:** **"We offer a 30-day money back guarantee on your first order from us."** Per brand messaging, if a customer isn't satisfied, SightSage will refund their money or send a replacement supplement.
- **Refund Policy:** A formal refund/return policy is published at `/policies/refund-policy`, with return conditions outlined in the FAQ.
- **Free Shipping:** Free shipping on orders over **$99** (USA & Canada).
- **Vision Club:** A point-based membership/loyalty savings program.

### Coverage Notes

The richest brand-authority content lives on the **Who We Are** and **Our Founder** pages. Quality/certification specifics (GMP/NSF, sourcing, guarantee) are confirmed across product pages and corroborating sources; the brand's own self-FAQ lists these as topics but several answer bodies were not exposed in the fetched markdown. The University of Waterloo trial and ClinicalTrials.gov ID (NCT05027087) are the brand's headline credibility signals.
