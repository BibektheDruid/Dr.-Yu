### JS-rendering / accessibility notes

- **Reviews are NOT blocked by JS rendering on the SightSage storefront.** Across the homepage, the dedicated `/pages/reviews` page, and every individual product page fetched (SightC, Blueberry Gummies, Hair Pro, Superfood Wellness Blend, Adaptogen-X, Blueberry & Veggie Cookies), the Judge.me rating, review counts, star breakdowns, and verbatim review text were all present in the static HTML and fully extractable via WebFetch. No JS execution was required.
- **What IS limited:** product pages typically surface only the **first few reviews** statically (roughly 4–5 per page); the remaining reviews behind "load more" / pagination are fetched by the Judge.me widget on scroll. Full review-by-review enumeration beyond the first page would require a JS-capable browser (e.g., Playwright) or the Judge.me API. Aggregate rating and total count, however, are always available statically.
- **Recurring reviewers:** A small set of names (Dawn S., Kiki, Sukh B., Christine) appear across multiple products, suggesting a core of repeat/loyal customers (and possibly some incentivized reviewing) rather than a broad independent base.
- **Coverage gaps:** Several higher-priced bundles and the $298 Hou Kui Black Tea and $568 herbal program show **0 reviews** — review depth is concentrated in the four eye/wellness bestsellers, which account for ~545 of the 653 total reviews.
- **Off-site context:** A third-party aggregator (sightsage.myprosandcons.com) lists a much lower 4.0/5 — but that is based on a single review and is not representative. No major independent platform (Trustpilot, etc.) surfaced a substantial SightSage review corpus; Judge.me on the brand's own site is effectively the sole review source.
