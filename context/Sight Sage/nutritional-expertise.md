## Nutritional Expertise & Educational Content

SightSage (sightsage.com) backs its supplement brand with a substantial library of educational content spanning eye-health science, dry-eye and digital-lifestyle guidance, TCM-rooted wellness, and nutrition. The content is organized across three main hubs — the **News blog** (founder story, TCM/clinical content, patient outcomes), the **Eye Health blog** (the deepest educational/nutritional vertical), and the **Lifestyle blog** (broader wellness: weight, hair, stress, digestion) — and is anchored by two named experts who establish the brand's authority.

### Expertise & Authority Positioning

SightSage positions its credibility on a two-founder partnership combining Western/clinical nutrition with Traditional Chinese Medicine:

**Dr. Weidong Yu — Doctor of Traditional Chinese Medicine (the clinical authority)**
- Bachelor of Medicine, Shandong College of Traditional Chinese Medicine (1983), with five years of combined TCM and Western medicine study.
- Master's in Public Health, Free University of Brussels, Belgium (1991).
- Licensed Acupuncturist (California); Registered Doctor of TCM (British Columbia).
- Marketed as having "more than 43 years of experience (1983–2026)," with practice across China, Belgium, the US, and Canada.
- Former Assistant Research Fellow at the China Academy of Traditional Chinese Medicine (Beijing); author of 30+ medical publications; invited by the **WHO in 2015** as an international expert on acupuncture clinical practice.
- Described as "world-renowned" and "renowned for treating retinitis pigmentosa (RP)" patients. Formulator of the Blueberry Gummies line.

**Kathy (Zhang/Yu) — "KathyHealthTips" (the nutrition/dietetics authority)**
- Positioned as a **Registered Dietitian** and social-media health educator ("KathyHealthTips").
- Also described as a certified acupuncturist with 30+ years in TCM, co-managing the clinic.
- Co-formulator of SightC; the bridge that makes TCM "accessible through education and practical wellness guidance."

**Institutional / clinical proof points used for authority:**
- **Wellspring Clinic** (founded 1997, Vancouver) specializing in Retinitis Pigmentosa and retinal conditions via "integrated TCM and modern vision assessment."
- A recurring **"20+ years of clinical research"** / "30+ years combined clinical experience" framing.
- Brand mission: **"Sight for Humanity,"** emphasizing whole-body health over isolated symptom treatment (TCM's interconnected-systems perspective).
- An on-site **OSDI (Ocular Surface Disease Index) dry-eye self-test** is used as a clinical-style engagement/credibility tool.

### Key Educational Themes

**1. Dry-eye & digital-lifestyle education (the strongest, most repeated vertical).** Consistent, practical advice tied directly to screen use and (often) localized to Vancouver:
- Mechanism: screens reduce blink rate (~50% less; normal ~15–20 blinks/min) and cause incomplete blinks, reducing tear distribution and lubrication.
- Signature advice: the **20-20-20 rule**, conscious blinking, screen positioning below eye level, larger fonts, reduced glare/brightness, hydration, and humidifiers.
- Environmental aggravators: air conditioning, heating, low humidity, fans/vents, poor lighting; distinguishes transient *screen fatigue* from persistent *dry eye disease* and flags when to see a professional.

**2. Nutrition for vision (ingredient-led science).** Educational pieces double as soft positioning for the product nutrients:
- **Blueberries / anthocyanins** — antioxidant/anti-inflammatory protection of retinal blood vessels; cited benefits for night vision, dark adaptation, cataracts, retinopathy, AMD, and dry eye; plus vitamins C/K and manganese.
- **Goji berries / zeaxanthin & carotenoids** — cites a randomized pilot trial showing increased macular pigment optical density (MPOD) and blue-light filtration, with an honest caveat that larger studies are needed.
- Recurring "eye-supporting nutrients": **lutein, zeaxanthin, vitamin A, omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants** (goji, turmeric, dandelion).

**3. Environmental & lifestyle eye health.** Air pollution and eye health (particulate matter, ozone, NO2/SO2 linked to dry eye, glaucoma, retinopathy, AMD; protective tips include antioxidants, hydration, wraparound sunglasses, air purifiers); fluorescent lighting; eye-rubbing habits; pediatric/child screen protection; eye floaters; and mind-body links (stress/burnout and depression affecting vision).

**4. TCM & clinical vision treatment (authority/testimonial content).** RP-focused case stories and outcomes (e.g., "field of view expanded fivefold to 200 degrees," restored sight) — these lean on patient testimonials and Dr. Yu's clinic reputation rather than published mechanisms.

**5. Broader wellness (Lifestyle blog).** Weight management (insulin resistance, Ozempic taste changes), hair-growth nutrition ("your hair shows what you eat"), hormonal health (PCOS, menopause), digestion/bloating, fatty-liver herbal support, and stress/adaptogen content — extending the nutrition-and-TCM authority beyond eyes.

### Positioning Summary
SightSage's educational content is deliberately **dual-authority**: Dr. Yu supplies deep TCM/clinical legitimacy (RP treatment, WHO recognition, 40+ years, Wellspring Clinic), while Kathy/KathyHealthTips supplies registered-dietitian nutrition credibility and an accessible social-media voice. Most educational articles are **ingredient- and habit-led** (blueberry/goji nutrients, the 20-20-20 rule, dry-eye habits) that function simultaneously as genuine wellness education and as soft justification for the product line (SightC, Blueberry Gummies, dry-eye bundles, the "Wellness Tips for Digital Lifestyle" product). Notably, nutritional/lifestyle articles cite general research and pilot studies (often with honest caveats), whereas the TCM/RP authority content relies primarily on testimonials and clinic reputation rather than published mechanisms.
